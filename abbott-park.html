Overview

This dataset comprises over 150 thousand enterprises registered with the City of Los Angeles' Office of Finance. A business in Los Angeles must apply for a Business Tax Registration Certificate (BTRC). Each business is assigned a location account number, a legal business name, a doing business as (DBA) name, a location, a mailing address, an industry code, a start date, and so on.

Dataset Details

Every person and organization conducting business inside the City of Los Angeles must apply for a Business Tax Registration Certificate (BTRC) and pay annual business taxes at the City Office of Finance. This rule is applicable to any company that has a physical location inside the city borders or whose owner or staff does business there. For a new business registration, the following details are required: Legal Business Name, Business Name/Doing Business As, Business Start Date, Business Type, Mailing Address, Business Contact Information, Sales Tax Number, and Business/Professional Activity Code. The business activity professions include those in manufacturing, radio, television, cable, multimedia, motion picture and sound recording, real estate & rental & leasing, services, telecommunications and utilities, transportation, and warehousing. They also include those in the arts, entertainment, and recreation, construction, finance, insurance, food establishments and sales, health care, and social assistance. This collection contains both active and inactive companies and trades. Each business has registered its location account number, legal business name, doing business as (DBA) name, business address, postal address, industry code, start date, and end date, among other details. The City of Los Angeles maintains a dedicated Open Data Portal website that provides immediate access to real-time data that is updated often, fostering innovation and raising the standard of city services. The City's data repository, the Open Data Portal, was developed to encourage the free exchange of information and advance democracy through unconventional community participation strategies including data sharing.
